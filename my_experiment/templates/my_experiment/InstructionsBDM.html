Welcome to the bidding part

In this part, you will tell us how much you want each snack.

You will choose one price for each snack.

Your price can be anywhere from $0.00 to $1.00.

How to choose a price

Each snack has a slider.

You can either drag the slider or click on the bar to choose your price.

This is an example screen:

The number below the slider shows your price.

Choose the most you would be willing to pay for that snack.

You can choose in steps of $0.01.

What to do

This is an example screen:

Choose one price for every snack on the page.

When you are done, click Submit Bids.

You will then see the result on the screen.

After that, the Next button will appear so you can continue.

Make sure you are happy with your answers before you submit.

How the result is decided

After you submit, the computer will randomly pick one snack.

Then it will randomly choose a price for that snack.

If your bid is the same as or higher than that price, you get the snack and pay that random price.

If your bid is lower than that price, you do not get the snack.

You pay the random price (not your bid) out of your $5.00 CAD show-up fee; the rest of that show-up fee stays with you.

Example: if you bid $0.20 and the drawn price is $0.10, you get the snack and keep $4.90 of your $5.00 show-up fee.

Why you should bid honestly

Your bid should be the most you truly think the snack is worth to you.

You do not pay your own bid.

If you get the snack, you pay the random price chosen by the computer.

Because of this, the best choice is to enter your real price for each snack.

Why guessing higher or lower does not help

If you bid higher than your real price, you might end up getting a snack for more than you really wanted to pay.

If you bid lower than your real price, you might miss a chance to get a snack that you would have wanted at that price.

So the best choice is simple: bid what the snack is truly worth to you.

Take a moment

You can use the arrow buttons to review these instruction pages at any time.

Make sure everything is clear before moving on.

When you feel ready, click Next to start a practice round.
